What Is an AI Image Generator and How Does It Work?

AI image generation takes your text and turns it into a picture. You type "red sports car on a mountain road at sunset," the model interprets the words, and out comes a visual. Under the hood, it's diffusion models doing the heavy lifting ‚Äî starting with noise and refining it step-by-step until the image matches your description.

Here's the pipeline:

  • Text Prompt ‚Üí You enter what you want (e.g., "Cyberpunk city").
  • Text Encoder/Tokenizer ‚Üí Converts your words into numerical embeddings the model can read, using models like CLIP (Runware Docs, 2025).
  • Latent Space (Noise) ‚Üí Generation kicks off with random noise in a compressed representation to save processing power (ComfyUI, 2025).
  • Denoising Steps ‚Üí The model iteratively cleans up the noise, guided by your text embeddings. More steps typically equal more detail but take longer (Hugging Face, 2025).
  • Final Image ‚Üí After the denoising loop wraps, the latent data is decoded into the finished picture (Runware Docs, 2025).

Modern diffusion models like Stable Diffusion and SDXL work in latent space for speed. Instead of manipulating every single pixel from the start, text gets tokenized via pre-trained models (CLIP ViT-L/14, for example). Then, cross-attention mechanisms align the visual output with your semantic input, step by step, ensuring the result matches your prompt (pcnews.ru, 2023; habr.com, 2023; ultralytics.com, 2023).

Scientific Basis & Research

The technology is grounded in "High-Resolution Image Synthesis with Latent Diffusion Models" (Rombach et al., 2022), which introduced method of training in latent space to reduce computational costs while maintaining quality. Additionally, advancements like the "DALL¬∑E 3 System Card" (OpenAI, 2023) focus on improving prompt fidelity and rendering text within images.

Secrets of Prompt Crafting: Formulas and Examples

Negative prompts

Exclude artifacts like: bad anatomy, blurry, extra limbs, deformed hands, watermark.

Legal and Ethical Aspects of Using AI Art

‚öñÔ∏è Copyright: Who Owns the Rights?

Rely on the specific service's Terms of Service (ToS), but understand the overarching legal framework. Check limitations on training datasets and brand usage restrictions.

Key Legal Rulings (2025 Update)

1. Human Authorship Required: Fully AI-generated content cannot be copyrighted in the United States. This was reaffirmed by the U.S. Court of Appeals in Thaler v. Perlmutter (March 2025) and the USCO's Part 2 Report (Jan 2025).

2. Human Input Exception: Works incorporating significant human creative input (editing, arranging, selecting elements) may qualify for copyright, but protection applies only to the human-created portions, not the raw AI output (U.S. Copyright Office, 2025).

3. Training Data & Infringement: The U.S. Copyright Office concluded in its Part 3 Report (May 2025) that using copyrighted works to train AI models typically constitutes reproduction. This may be considered prima facie infringement and fall outside "fair use" protections, particularly if the AI outputs closely resemble and compete with the original works in the marketplace.

Practical Application:

You can't copyright a raw DALL¬∑E 3 output. However, if you take that image and composite it into a video edit with original footage, music, and voiceover, the final video qualifies for copyright as a derivative work with human authorship.

Source: USCO AI Report Part 3 (May 2025)
