What Is an AI Image Generator and How Does It Work?

AI image generation takes your text and turns it into a picture. You type "red sports car on a mountain road at sunset," the model interprets the words, and out comes a visual. Under the hood, it's diffusion models doing the heavy lifting — starting with noise and refining it step-by-step until the image matches your description.

THere's the pipeline:

  • Text Prompt → You enter what you want.
  • Text Encoder/Tokenizer → Converts your words into numerical embeddings the model can read (Runware Docs, 2025). 
  • Latent Space (Noise) → Generation kicks off with random noise in a compressed representation (ComfyUI, 2025).  
  • Denoising Steps → The model iteratively cleans up the noise, guided by your text. More steps = more detail, but also more time (Hugging Face, 2025).  
  • Final Image → After denoising wraps, you get the finished picture (Runware Docs, 2025).

Modern diffusion models like Stable Diffusion and SDXL work in latent space for speed. Text gets tokenized via pre-trained models (CLIP ViT-L/14, for example), then cross-attention mechanisms align the visual output with your semantic input, step by step (pcnews.ru, 2023; habr.com, 2023; ultralytics.com, 2023).

How to Create the Perfect Image: 3 Simple Steps

1

Write a detailed image prompt.

Specify purpose, composition, angle, lighting, style, image text (if you need text on a poster), materials. Add an image description with key attributes and constraints for an exact image. Example: [Subject] + [Details] + [Environment] + [Style] "Red sports car (subject), matte finish, aerodynamic curves (details), mountain road at sunset (environment), cinematic photography style (style), 35mm lens, shallow depth of field (camera/lighting), no visible brand logos (constraints)."

2

Upload a reference image.

Guide style/pose/palette. Set the influence strength of the reference and choose single image or variation series.

3

Set parameters and generate.

Define aspect ratio, steps (20–50), CFG (7–12), seed (random or locked); hit generate image. Compare image results, refine your prompt, and lock in successful settings for repeatability. Pro tip: Add negative prompts to exclude common errors: `bad anatomy, blurry, extra limbs, deformed hands, watermark, low quality, text artifacts`.

Secrets of Prompt Crafting: Formulas and Examples

Ideal prompt formula:

[Object] + [Details] + [Environment] + [Style] + [Technique] + [Constraints]

Example: "Red sports car (object), matte finish, aerodynamic curves (details), mountain road at sunset (environment), cinematic photography style (style), 35mm lens, shallow depth of field (technique), no visible brand logos (constraints)."

Negative prompts: Exclude artifacts like "bad anatomy, blurry, extra limbs, deformed hands, watermark."

Weighting words: Use parentheses (keyword) or brackets [keyword] to emphasize; some tools support numerical weights like (keyword:1.5).
